My friend put together a plot in a TTRPG that we're playing through, and here are the notes I made - I think he's made inference to real life stuff (like a theory on power or something) but I'm not sure. Three mini-bosses hold power in three different ways: Divine right Technology * Legend, or fable, or tradition, or something like that Is there a theory of power (or autocracy, or government, etc.) that maps cleanly onto these characters?

Short answer: there isnt a super-famous Divine Right / Technology / Legend triad with those exact labelsbut what your friends doing lines up really neatly with a mash-up of a few big ideas about power:

  • Webers three types of authority
  • Technocracy / rule by experts
  • Control of myth, culture, and tradition (cultural hegemony / soft power)

Let me map them in a way thats usable for you in the game.

1. Divine Right Sacred / Charismatic Authority
...

This boss rules because the gods say so (or some equivalent supernatural mandate).

That screams:

  • Divine right of kings in early modern Europe.
  • Max Weber: Charismatic authority power justified by belief in someones special, chosen status (prophet, messiah, miracle-worker).
  • Also partly traditional authority if its a hereditary priest-king line (weve always done it this way, blessed by the gods).

Theme in play:
People obey not because its efficient or because of laws, but because disobeying feels like blasphemy or cosmic treason.

2. Technology Technocratic / RationalLegal Power
...

This boss rules because they control knowledge, infrastructure, and tools:

  • Advanced machines, networks, surveillance, maybe magical tech.
  • Specialists and engineers who keep everything running.

That maps cleanly to:

  • Technocracy rule by experts / engineers / scientists.
  • Webers rationallegal authority power justified by rules, bureaucracy, systems, and claims of being the most rational or efficient.

Theme in play:
People obey because the system works (or appears to): the trains run on time, the food grows, the lights stay on. If you cross this boss, they flip switches, shut down your access, or weaponize the infrastructure.

3. Legend / Fable / Tradition Mythic / Traditional Authority
...

This boss rules because of stories:

  • Founding myths (Our ancestor slew the dragon and earned us this valley.)
  • Cultural traditions (We always bow to the Bear King at midwinter.)
  • Prophecies, songs, and folktales that make their rule feel inevitable.

That matches:

  • Webers Traditional authority power because thats the way its always been.
  • Cultural hegemony (Gramsci) control over the stories and norms so people internalize the rulers legitimacy.
  • Soft power influence through culture and narrative, not just force.

Theme in play:
People obey because their entire identity and history are wrapped around this ruler or dynasty. To rebel is to betray your ancestors, your story, your sense of who you are.

So is there a clean named theory here?
...

Not one neat, brand-name theory with those exact three wordsbut:

  • Divine Right sacred/charismatic authority
  • Technology rationallegal authority / technocracy
  • Legend/Tradition traditional authority / cultural hegemony

Put differently, your friend has basically arranged three sources of legitimacy:

  1. The gods / the transcendent I rule because of higher, cosmic will.
  2. The system / expertise I rule because I can make things work.
  3. The story / the past I rule because history and tradition say I should.

Thats a very political-theory-brain way to design bosses, so your instinct is probably right: theyre almost certainly riffing on real-world theories of how power justifies itself.
